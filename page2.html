    People draw for a variety of reasons, and it can serve as an emotional support or a source of income.

    1. Emotional Outlet – Drawing can be a way to express feelings or experiences that are difficult to articulate with words. Creativity – It provides a means to explore and showcase one's imagination and unique perspectives.
    2. Therapeutic – For many, drawing is a calming activity that helps to reduce stress and anxiety. Mindfulness – Like coloring, drawing can help individuals focus and be present in the moment, helping to clear the mind
    3. Visual Storytelling – Drawing is a way to tell stories or convey messages through images, whether it’s a comic, illustration, or design. Artistic Documentation – Some people draw to document their observations of the world around them
    4. Learning and Growth – Drawing helps develop fine motor skills, spatial awareness, and attention to detail. Building Technical Ability – Artists often draw to improve their technique and understanding of form, shadow, perspective, and anatomy.
    5. Visualizing Ideas – People use drawing to brainstorm, plan projects, or visualize concepts, especially in fields like architecture, engineering, or design. Creative Solutions – Drawing can help find solutions to problems by mapping out options and possibilities.
    6. Hobby or Passion – For many, drawing is simply a fun and enjoyable activity they do in their free time. Escaping Reality – It allows people to create their own world and explore different scenarios or fantasies
    7. Therapeutic Art – Art therapy uses drawing as a way to heal trauma, process grief, or cope with mental health challenges. Gift or Tribute – People draw to create meaningful gifts for others or to pay tribute to someone special. Building Community – Sharing drawings with others (whether on social media or in a group) can foster connection and inspire others.
